One afternoon, near the sandy river bank    
I met a creature of the strangest kind    
You might mistake it for a huge roly-poly,    
at the very first glance.    
But instead of crawling or rolling into a ball,    
upright it stands!    
Look at its many, I mean MANY hands:    
each sits on an arm    
that is soft and flaccid like a rubber band    
oh, the finger, which looks like a party blow out    
only one such finger on each hand:    
when curled up it looks like zero    
when blew out it looks like one    

Where does it come from?    
Is it another planet like Saturn    
or another country like Sudan?    
This thought reminded myself to go home    
as I ran, the creature followed behind    
Wait, is it too lonely    
or it treats me as a friend?    
Maybe I can bring it home    
maybe I can help it to reunite with its own kind    
So I did and learned so much about the creature    
that surprised me again and again    

It doesn’t talk our talk but it know signs    
when a door closed, it extended its finger zero    
when the door opened, it extended its finger one.    
Same with the flag on the mail box:    
when the flag was down, its finger zero    
when the flag up, its finger one    
As I doing things and going places    
it kept changing its fingers and hands    
as if it understands    
as if it’s trying to communicate with me    
with patterns of zero and ones    

So I went on to test my hypothesis by experiment:    
I extended three of my fingers    
The creature did the same with three of its hands    
each with the finger one extended    
perfectly aligned along a straight line    
Then I showed it five pieces of pecans    
It responded with five hands    
each with the finger one extended    
perfectly aligned along a straight line    


The strangest thing is yet to come    
as I later find    
One day when I check on it
